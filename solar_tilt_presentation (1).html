Introduction

Why Tilt Optimization Actually Matters

🌇
Urban Heat Island Effect
Dense urban environments raise local temperatures, compounding thermal losses in solar panels beyond standard STC predictions.
📐
Rule-of-Thumb Installations
Most Indian rooftop installations are set at fixed tilt angles decided by installers using approximations — not field-validated data.
☀️
Seasonal Declination Shift
The optimal tilt angle migrates with the sun's arc between solstices. A single fixed angle sacrifices significant yield across seasons.
⚡
Scale Impact
Even a 5–10° correction in panel tilt, calibrated to local conditions, can meaningfully improve annual energy yield. Across thousands of Bengaluru rooftop installations — that difference is enormous.
🔬
Zero Local Field Data
No empirical, hardware-driven dataset exists for Bengaluru's specific coordinates and microclimate. We're generating it.

Literature Review

Four Critical Research Gaps

GAP 01
Almost No Real Hardware in India
Published reviews confirm that nearly all Indian tilt-optimization studies are purely computational. Real-time sensor-based field experiments are virtually absent in the literature.
GAP 02
Bengaluru Has No Field-Validated Data
Mathematical models estimate 11.4°–26.7° as optimal for tropical cities — but none are validated at Bengaluru's actual microclimate coordinates. Nobody has measured it.
GAP 03
Urban Heat Island + Thermal Correction Uncombined
Research acknowledges UHI degrades panel efficiency, but no study simultaneously combines real-time temperature correction with tilt optimization as a concurrent measurement.
GAP 04
Intraday Tilt Behavior Ignored
All existing papers give monthly or seasonal optimal angles. Nobody has measured what the best angle is at 8am vs 12pm vs 4pm on the same day — we will.
💡 Our approach: We address all four gaps simultaneously — real hardware, Bengaluru-specific, thermal + tilt combined, intraday resolution. Validated against existing theoretical models for double novelty.

Objectives

What We're Measuring

📡

Build & Deploy a Multi-Sensor Data Acquisition System

Construct a hardware system with solar cell, LDR/BH1750, MPU-6050 gyroscope, and DS18B20 temperature sensors — all simultaneously logging timestamped data.

📐

Test Three Controlled Tilt Angles Across a Full Solar Day

Compare power output at low, moderate, and steep inclinations from dawn to dusk in defined intervals — building complete intraday power curves.

🌡️

Apply Temperature-Correction Algorithm to Isolate Geometric Efficiency

Remove thermal derating bias (~0.45%/°C) from raw power readings to measure pure panel efficiency independent of ambient temperature effects.

📊

Validate Empirical Findings Against Theoretical Models

Cross-reference collected field data with Liu-Jordan and HDKR computational predictions to establish how well existing models hold for Bengaluru's microclimate.

Methodology

Sensor Architecture

Sensor Parameter Measured Role in Study
Solar Cell Sensor
PV Transducer
 Voltage × Current = Power Output (W) Primary dependent variable — measures actual energy yield at each tilt configuration
Ambient Light Sensor
LDR / BH1750
 Irradiance in Lux — independent of panel Normalizes for cloud cover and scattering; isolates panel efficiency from raw sunlight availability
Tilt / Gyroscope Sensor
MPU-6050
 Angular position in degrees (6-axis) Provides verified tilt configuration for every data point — eliminates angular estimation error
Temperature Sensor
DS18B20
 Panel surface + ambient temperature (°C) Enables thermal derating correction: removes ~0.45%/°C efficiency loss bias from power readings
All four sensors feed simultaneously into a microcontroller which timestamps every data point — producing a multi-dimensional dataset impossible with any single sensor alone.
